A little About Me

I was born in Longview, Washington, in 1981. By the end of '81, my Dad and I moved to Anchorage, Alaska. Whether I'd describe it as a "pleasure" living there until 1992 is debatable. If you plan on visiting Alaska, my advice is to fly into Anchorage and leave right away—it's not the best city! When my Dad and I returned to Washington in '92, we settled in one of the best small towns to grow up in: Castle Rock, WA, with a population of 2,300.

In my teenage years, I played hockey for the Portland Jr. Hawks, an experience that had a profound impact on me, with memories that will last a lifetime.

Post high school, I made my parents proud by attending one semester of Community College. However, I dropped out and moved to Mt. Hood to pursue my passion for snowboarding.

After a couple of seasons, I moved back home, started working at Bruno's Pizza, and met my wife, Sarah. Now, we've been going strong for over 21 years!

Around 2003 or 2004, I reluctantly joined my family's Well Drilling business, despite initially vowing never to work there.

In January 2007, my daughter Sienna was born, and in July 2008, my son Zeppelin arrived. Then came August 2008, when my dad had to lay me off due to the economic downturn caused by the Global Financial Crisis (GFC).

I went back to school, worked at Bruno's, and took on side jobs to make ends meet—an interesting time, to say the least. The turning point in my current story happened one night at Bruno's Pizza. Fed up with the grind, I picked up a newspaper during a break to look for job opportunities. Almost like a sign, Steelscape LLC was hiring. I started the application process the next day.

On May 3rd, 2011, I began working at Steelscape. Since then, my family and I have never looked back. I dedicated myself to the job, learning every position, and fully immersing myself in our ERP System (SAP). In 2014, I led the execution of a significant project, returning fifty leased railcars, saving us over $250,000 by completing the project in-house in less than four months.

After my two-year term as a Coordinator, I started working on projects throughout the business, not just for Steelscape but also for our sister company ASC Profiles.

In November 2016, I transitioned into a Continuous Improvement Role for CPNA, leading or assisting with various initiatives. On January 2nd, 2019, I became one of the founding team members of a newly developed corporate department called Manufacturing Excellence.

Then came the pandemic, and our department faced challenges with no travel. Undeterred, I found new ways to contribute by helping people learn to work remotely. I collaborated with our IT Team to host webinars on using Microsoft Teams, then expanded into MS Enterprise 365 Products like OneDrive, OneNote, ToDo, and Planner. I also delved into Power BI, learning to build enterprise-level reports through various methods.

Fast forward to the present day—my boss recently asked if I'd be interested in taking a Professional Certificate in Coding: Full Stack Development with MERN through MIT | xPRO. So here I am, once again learning something new, far outside my comfort zone.
